The ratio soared from 104% in 2017 to 123% in 2024. Dalio warns we're approaching an inflection point where debt service costs crowd out productive spending — the beginning of the "big cycle" turning.

Dalio argues the deficit must come down to ~3% of GDP to avoid a crisis. The current 7% is more than double that threshold and projected to reach 9% by 2034 according to Moody's.

Interest payments trajectory

Billions USD

Annual interest on federal debt crossed $1 trillion in 2024 and could approach $2 trillion by 2028 if rates stay elevated — consuming an ever-larger share of government revenue and crowding out all other spending.

The death spiral mechanism

Dalio's self-reinforcing loop
1
Rising deficits force the Treasury to issue more bonds to finance spending gaps
2
Bond oversupply overwhelms demand; investors demand higher yields as compensation
3
Rising yields increase the cost of servicing existing debt, widening the deficit further
4
Credit risk rises as ratings agencies and foreign buyers reassess U.S. creditworthiness
5
Capital flight — bonds sell off, dollar weakens, gold rises. Classic warning signals Dalio tracks
6
Fed prints money to backstop the market, debasing the currency and eroding real returns for bondholders
